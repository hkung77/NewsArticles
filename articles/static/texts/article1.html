Lou Williams is the NBA's sixth man of the year. The 28-year-old guard averaged a career-best 15.5 points per game for the Raptors, appearing in 80 of 82 games and helping Toronto to a franchise-record 49 wins. He got 78 first-place votes from a panel of 130 writers and broadcasters who cover the NBA regularly and becomes the first Raptor to win ever win the award. Read more at the Toronto Star.
